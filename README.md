# 🏺 Helmholtz Amorph Scare Machine
### *Industrial Additive Synthesis for the Cmajor Ecosystem*

The **Helmholtz Amorph Scare Machine** is a 16-voice additive "Vossilator" designed for high-fidelity historical emulation. It recreates the scientific instability of 1905 laboratory equipment while integrating modern **Amorph** wave-folding and entropy-based decay.

---

## 🛠 Core DSP Features

### 1. Pythagorean Additive Engine
Unlike standard digital synths, this engine uses **Pythagorean Tension** logic. Each of the 10 harmonics per voice is offset by a minute transcendental curve, creating a "heavy," wooden resonance that feels physical rather than mathematical.
* **Harmonics:** 160 total oscillators (16 voices × 10).
* **Pitch:** Calibrated to 256 Hz (Scientific Pitch).

### 2. The Amorph Chain (Non-Linear Decay)
* **Voltage Sag:** Simulates power supply struggle. High polyphony causes the virtual voltage to "brown out," dropping the pitch and amplitude.
* **Amorph Fold:** A non-linear saturation stage. When signals exceed the sagging voltage, they fold back into the spectrum, creating aggressive, metallic "shrieks."
* **Era Filter:** A sliding bandwidth-limiting algorithm mapping 1900 (Wax Cylinder) to 2026 (Hi-Fi).

### 3. Thermal Calibration
Triggering the `calibrate` event initiates a 5-second mechanical warm-up. The oscillators sweep through their harmonic series to "seat" the virtual resonators, ensuring authentic pitch drift and character.

## 🚀 Installation & Usage
1. Open **GitHub Codespaces** in your browser.
2. Ensure `HelmholtzAmorphScare.cmajor` and `manifest.json` are in the root directory.
3. Load the patch into any Cmajor-compatible host (Amorph DAW).
4. **Tip:** Always hit the `Calibrate` button after loading to "warm up" the virtual hardware.
